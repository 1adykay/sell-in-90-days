Your Seller To-Do List

Everything you need to do — in order.

This is your realistic, no-fluff checklist. I'll walk through every item with you during our free walkthrough — but start here so you know what's coming.

1
Before We List — Preparation
Weeks 1–2 · Do these before anything goes on the market
Deep clean the entire home — top to bottom
Windows, baseboards, grout, appliances, light fixtures. Buyers notice everything. This costs nothing but time and makes a massive difference.
🔥 High impact
Declutter every room — ruthlessly
Remove personal photos, excess furniture, knick-knacks, and anything that makes rooms feel small. Rent a storage unit if needed. Buyers need to see the home, not your stuff.
🔥 High impact
Paint over any bold or unusual wall colors
Neutral tones only — white, greige, soft gray. Fresh paint is one of the highest ROI improvements you can make. A gallon costs $40 and can add thousands to your sale price.
🔥 High impact · ~$200–600
Fix all minor repairs — squeaky doors, leaky faucets, broken fixtures
Buyers make mental lists of everything wrong. Each item they notice becomes a negotiation chip against your price. Fix the small stuff before they see it.
💚 Medium impact · Low cost
Maximize curb appeal — mow, edge, mulch, plant flowers
First impressions happen before buyers walk in the door. Mow the lawn, edge the driveway, add fresh mulch, plant a few colorful annuals at the entry. Under $100 and it shows up in every photo.
🔥 High impact · ~$50–150
Deep clean or replace carpet if heavily worn
Professional carpet cleaning is $100–200 and can make old carpet look nearly new. If it's beyond saving, consider replacement or offering a flooring credit.
💚 Medium impact
Update light fixtures and hardware if dated
Brass fixtures from 1995 scream "old house." Budget brushed nickel or matte black fixtures from Home Depot run $15–40 each and modernize a room instantly.
🔥 High impact · ~$50–200
Make sure all lights work and use matching bulbs
Daylight bulbs (5000K) make rooms feel bright and clean. Mismatched or burned-out bulbs signal neglect. Replace every single one before photos.
💚 Medium impact · ~$20–40

Small Effort. Big Return.

The tweaks that add thousands to your price.

You don't need a full renovation to get more money. These are the small, low-cost improvements that consistently deliver the highest ROI in Ohio's market — especially in rural and suburban properties.

💡

The Rule: Spend $1 to Make $3

Every improvement on this list has a proven return that exceeds the cost. The goal isn't to renovate — it's to strategically spend $500–2,000 to net $5,000–20,000 more at closing. I'll tell you exactly which ones apply to your home.

$1,000–5,000+
Average added value

Fresh Paint Throughout

Nothing transforms a home faster or cheaper than a fresh coat of paint in neutral tones. Buyers see fresh paint as a move-in ready signal — and they'll pay for it.

Stick to white, greige, or light gray throughout
Don't forget ceilings, trim, and doors
Exterior paint adds serious curb appeal if peeling
One accent wall in a bedroom is fine — bold throughout is not
Cost: $200–800 DIY / $800–2,500 professional
$3,000–10,000+
Average added value

Kitchen Quick Wins

The kitchen sells the house. You don't need new cabinets — you need updated hardware, clean surfaces, and modern fixtures. Buyers mentally live in the kitchen first.

Replace cabinet hardware ($2–5/knob, massive visual impact)
Paint cabinets white or navy if dated wood tone
Replace faucet with a modern brushed nickel ($40–100)
Deep clean or replace grout between tile
Add under-cabinet lighting if budget allows (~$50–150)
Cost: $100–1,500 depending on scope
$2,000–8,000+
Average added value

Bathroom Refresh

Dated bathrooms are buyer red flags. A few strategic updates — without a full remodel — can modernize the space and eliminate buyer hesitation completely.

Replace toilet seat, faucet, and towel bars ($80–200 total)
Re-caulk tub and shower — fresh caulk looks brand new
Replace vanity light fixture with modern style ($30–80)
Paint vanity if dark wood — white or navy are buyer favorites
Replace toilet if old or stained (~$120–200 installed)
Cost: $150–600 per bathroom
$5,000–15,000+
Average added value

Curb Appeal & Entry

Buyers decide how they feel about a home before they unlock the front door. Your exterior and entry set the emotional tone for the entire showing — make it count.

Power wash driveway, sidewalks, and siding ($50–200)
Paint or replace the front door — bold color makes a statement
New house numbers, doorknob, and mailbox ($30–80 total)
Fresh mulch in all beds ($80–200)
Seasonal flowers at entry — $20 at Lowe's, infinite impact
Outdoor lighting — well-lit entry feels safe and welcoming
Cost: $150–500 for full curb appeal overhaul
$2,000–7,000+
Average added value

Flooring Updates

Flooring is one of the first things buyers notice and one of the most common objections. You don't always need to replace — but you always need to address it.

Professional carpet cleaning ($100–200) before replacement
Replace worn carpet with LVP flooring if budget allows
Refinish hardwood floors if dull or scratched (~$2–4/sqft)
Remove carpet over hardwood — often reveals a selling point
Cost: $100–3,000 depending on scope
Huge for rural properties
Specific to Ohio rural homes

Rural Property Specific

Rural Ohio buyers care about things suburban buyers don't. These are the features that make your property stand out to the exact buyers I'm targeting for you.

Clean and organize the pole barn — staging applies outside too
Get well water tested — results in hand eliminate buyer fear
Get septic inspected proactively — big trust builder
Mow fields and clear fence lines — acreage looks bigger when managed
Document low utility costs — wood stove, well, septic monthly costs
Highlight outbuildings with measurements and uses
Cost: Mostly free — just effort and documentation
Removes buyer objections
Psychological selling points

Smell & Sensory Details

Buyers buy emotionally and justify rationally. What they smell and feel when they walk in sets the tone before they see a single room. Never underestimate this.

No pets during showings — remove animals and evidence
Bake cookies or diffuse a light, clean scent before showings
Air out the home before every showing — open windows when weather allows
Replace pet-stained carpet — this is non-negotiable if it smells
Keep temperature comfortable — 68–72°F during showings
Cost: Nearly free — pure preparation
Fastest growing buyer segment
Modern buyer expectations

Smart Home & Modern Touches

Remote workers and younger buyers specifically look for these. A few inexpensive upgrades signal "move-in ready" to the exact buyers paying top dollar right now.

Install a Nest or Ring thermostat (~$100–150, adds perceived value)
Add a Ring doorbell if you don't have one
Confirm internet speed and highlight it in marketing
Replace dated ceiling fans with modern styles ($40–80 each)
USB outlet plates in kitchen and primary bedroom ($12 each)
Cost: $100–400 total for full package
