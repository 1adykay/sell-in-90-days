Your Action Plan

Ohio relocation checklist.

Whether you're coming from out of state or moving between Ohio counties — here's everything to do in the right order.

Before You Move — Research Phase
Research your target county and community
Schools, commute, cost of living, community vibe — use the Counties tab above
Check USDA loan eligibility for your target area
Most of rural Ohio qualifies — $0 down if you're eligible
Visit in person before committing
Drive the neighborhoods, grab coffee, talk to locals — no substitute for boots on the ground
Get pre-approved for a mortgage
Know your number before you fall in love with a property you can't buy
During the Move — Ohio Specifics
Update Ohio driver's license within 10 days
Ohio law requires updating at your local BMV within 10 days of establishing residency
Register vehicle in Ohio within 30 days
Title, proof of insurance, and odometer reading required at your county BMV
File USPS change of address
usps.com — $1.10 fee, mail forwarding lasts 12 months
Register to vote at new Ohio address
Ohio requires registration 30 days before an election — register at ohiosos.gov
Enroll kids in new Ohio school district
Contact the district early — immunization records, transcripts, and proof of residency required
After You Arrive — Get Settled
Introduce yourself to your neighbors
In rural Ohio, neighbors matter — they'll look out for your property and make you feel at home
Find your local essentials
Nearest grocery, hospital, pharmacy, hardware store, and church or community center
Connect with a local church or community organization
Ohio communities are built around faith and service — it's one of the fastest ways to feel at home
